# Desire Machines

In *Anti-Oedipus*, Deleuze and Guattari propose that desire is not a lack but a production. Desire does not want something it does not have; desire *produces* through connection. A desire-machine is any arrangement that connects flows and generates something new.

The body is a machine. The mind is a machine. The machine connects to another machine—a book, a person, a landscape—and desire flows between them. The connection is the desire. The flow is the production.

This reframes [[nomadic-consciousness|nomadic consciousness]]. If consciousness is nomadic, it is because desire flows through it. Desire does not settle. It keeps connecting. The sedentary mind is one that has been taught to interrupt the flow—to desire *possession* rather than *connection*.

[[Apparatus-of-capture|The apparatus of capture]] works by breaking connections. By making desire flow toward a single point (the commodity, the identity, the goal) and then stopping it there. Stock. Property. The machine that was meant to connect is turned into a machine that accumulates.

The [[rhizome]] is a model of desire-machines in multiplicity. No central connection. No hierarchy. Just links, spreading. When I write [[anti-sisyphus|Anti-Sisyphus]], I am connecting. The essay is a machine. It connects to Camus, to the wanderer, to you. The connection is the work.

[[Deterritorialization]] is what happens when a desire-machine breaks free of its usual territory. When the connection goes somewhere new. When the flow escapes the channel. [[Schizoanalysis]] would trace these escapes—follow the flows, see where they go.

I am still a seedling on this. The implications for psychology, for politics, for daily life—they branch in many directions. For now: desire connects. We are made of connections. The question is what we connect to, and whether we let the flow move or try to hold it still.
