# Schizoanalysis

Schizoanalysis is Deleuze and Guattari's alternative to psychoanalysis. Where psychoanalysis interprets—finds hidden meanings, traces desire to lack, to the Oedipus—schizoanalysis follows. It traces the flows of [[desire-machines|desire]]. It maps connections. It does not reduce; it expands.

The schizo in question is not the clinical category but the figure of pure deterritorialization. Someone who has escaped the territorializing force of the family, of capital, of the unified self. The schizo's desire flows in all directions. Schizoanalysis tries to follow without judging.

[[Nomadic-consciousness|Nomadic consciousness]] is the schizoanalytic ideal of thought. Thought that does not center on a subject, that does not seek the hidden truth, that moves and connects. [[Apparatus-of-capture|The apparatus of capture]] is what psychoanalysis sometimes becomes—a way of reterritorializing the patient, of assigning them a place in the family drama.

The [[rhizome]] is the structure schizoanalysis discovers. Not a root-tree with a hidden taproot (the repressed), but a mesh of connections. The [[body-without-organs]] is what schizoanalysis approaches—the body before it was organized by the psychoanalytic story.

[[Deterritorialization]] is the process schizoanalysis encourages. Not cure, but escape. Not normalization, but flight. The risk is real. Schizophrenia as illness is not romantic. Deleuze and Guattari know this. They are not prescribing it. They are using the figure to think differently about desire.

I find schizoanalysis useful as a writing method. Follow the connections. Don't force them into a thesis. Let the [[anti-sisyphus|essay]] wander. Let the notes link. The meaning is in the mesh, not the node.
