# Body Without Organs

The body without organs (BwO) comes from Artaud. The body that has rid itself of organs—of the organization that makes it "function" in the service of the organism. Not a corpse. A body that has escaped its own organization. A surface. A field for intensities.

Deleuze and Guattari take up the concept. The BwO is what remains when we strip away the strata. When we stop being a "proper" body with proper functions. It is the body as potential. As capacity for connection. As the smooth surface across which [[desire-machines|desire]] flows.

[[Nomadic-consciousness|Nomadic consciousness]] could be described as a mind without organs—a mind that has escaped its usual organization. That does not process in the expected way. That connects freely. [[Deterritorialization]] is the process of becoming a BwO. Of losing our organization. Of becoming smooth.

The [[apparatus-of-capture|apparatus of capture]] works by imposing organization. By giving the body organs again. By making it functional, productive, legible. The BwO resists. It is the body that says: I will not be organized. I will be a surface for intensities only.

The [[rhizome]] has no center. The BwO has no hierarchy of organs. No brain commanding the rest. Just a flat field. [[Schizoanalysis]] would approach the BwO—follow the flows across it, see what connects.

We never achieve the BwO fully. We make them. We are always making a BwO—a body that can receive new intensities, new connections. The risk is the empty BwO—the one that has become catatonic, that receives nothing. The full BwO is crowded with flows. With [[anti-sisyphus|wandering]]. With connection.

To write is to make a BwO of the page. The page without fixed structure. The text that flows. The [[wikilink]] as a new organ—or the refusal of organs. A connection that does not subordinate.
