# Deterritorialization

Deterritorialization is the process of leaving. Of escaping a territory—whether that territory is geographic, psychic, linguistic, or conceptual. It is the moment when the coded becomes decoded. When the flow breaks free.

Deleuze and Guattari pair it with reterritorialization. We leave one territory only to enter another. Pure deterritorialization would be impossible—or fatal. We need somewhere to land. But the movement of leaving matters. It is the [[anti-sisyphus|wandering]] in miniature. The refusal to stay.

[[Nomadic-consciousness|Nomadic consciousness]] is deterritorialized thought. It does not hold territory. It passes through. The [[apparatus-of-capture|apparatus of capture]] exists to reterritorialize—to catch the escape, to assign it a new territory, to turn flight into property.

[[Desire-machines|Desire]] deterritorializes when it connects to something unexpected. When the usual object is abandoned for a new one. When the flow changes direction. The [[rhizome]] is deterritorialized structure—no center to hold.

The [[body-without-organs]] is the body in a state of maximum deterritorialization. Stripped of the organization that makes it a "proper" body. Smooth. Open. Vulnerable to new connections. [[Schizoanalysis]] would follow these lines of flight—see where the escape leads.

I think about writing as deterritorialization. The thought leaves the mind, enters the page. The page is a new territory. But if we hold it too tightly, it becomes a prison. The [[wikilink]] is a small deterritorialization—the text escapes into another text. The connection is the escape.

Nothing stays deterritorialized forever. The question is how long we can prolong the flight before capture. How far we can wander before the rock finds us again.
