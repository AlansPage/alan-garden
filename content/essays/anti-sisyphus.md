# Anti-Sisyphus: On the Wandering Spirit

## Preface

In man, a myriad of forces battle and seek to dominate, every impulse trying to break free, to overcome, to gain power, to unleash itself into the world without any restraints. The men of today, the men of endless debt and obligation, of endless toiling and endless struggle, do they seek their own captivity? Do they seek mountains and rocks to escape the vertigo of the steppe and the sea? Their own captivity - do they see salvation in it, or do they secretly despise it, seek ways out? This impulse to find ways out --- this trickster impulse --- where does it come from? A man of today can only laugh - a way out, an ESCAPE? Yet a thousand mirrors reflect only one face.

Every totalizing system has a tragedy that justifies its means. The Christian idea, the idea of salvation via the Holy Man, the idea *proto nihilismus* was spread by the archangels of its logic, Kant's categorical imperative and Hegel's dialectic. The Kingdom of Psychoanalysis has Oedipus, and its gate-guards Freud and Lacan. But what is Sisyphus? To be more precise --- what does Sisyphus justify? Isn't it an existential justification of captivity, a celebration --- no, a valorization and reverence towards the Tyrannical Impulse - the salvation-seeking impulse of all life-denial and life-restriction? Sisyphus must be overcome - Sisyphus, and it's becoming more and more clear to me, is one of the many justifying myths in our current totalizing system, the society of control, of algorithmics of capture. We have entered our age of Sisyphication, and many of us have justified Sisyphus to ourselves. This essay continues this line of thought with an example of nomadic cultures and the philosophical concept of *nomadicity*. To look at it as a cure and a solution would be too much. Perhaps, it's only a re-assemblage.

## Chapter 1: Dionysus vs. The Punished

### I. Active and Reactive Forces

To understand Sisyphus, we must first grasp the distinction between active and reactive forces---a distinction that Deleuze, in *Nietzsche and Philosophy*, positions at the heart of Nietzsche's revaluation of values. As Deleuze argues, active forces "always start with a 'yes' as they assert themselves and affirm difference while reactive forces scream 'no' in negation to the affirmation of difference" (p. 52). This is not a moral distinction but a qualitative one: "An active force is the stronger term and goes to the limit of what it can do. Its characteristics are dominating, possessing, subjugating and commanding" (p. 21), while reactive forces are defined by their relation to what dominates them.

Deleuze explains: "An action (which may be a thought, feeling, or practice) is active when it takes something as its object; conversely, it becomes reactive when it is made the object of someone or something else" (p. 34). The crucial insight: reactive forces do not create their own values but define themselves against external forces, needing what Nietzsche calls in *On the Genealogy of Morals* "an opposing, external world" to exist at all. As Nietzsche writes:

> slave morality says 'no' on principle to everything that is 'outside', 'other', 'non-self': and this 'no' is its creative deed. This reversal of the evaluating glance---this essential orientation to the outside instead of back onto itself---is a feature of ressentiment. (Nietzsche, *Genealogy*, Essay I, §10, p. 21)

The distinction matters because it reveals how forces can triumph not through superior strength but through capture. Reactive forces, as Deleuze notes, "always attempts to limit active forces by regulation and restriction as it is controlled by the spirit of the negative" [p. 64]. The question that animates both Nietzsche and Deleuze becomes: how do reactive forces, which are by definition weaker, come to dominate? How does negation capture affirmation?

### II. The Nomad and Smooth Space

In *A Thousand Plateaus*, Deleuze and Guattari develop the figure of the nomad not as a romanticized wanderer but as a principle of operation. Nomad space is "smooth," or open-ended: "One can rise up at any point and move to any other" (Deleuze & Guattari, *A Thousand Plateaus*, "Striated and Smooth Spaces," p. 320). This contrasts with State space, which is "striated"---"confined as by gravity to a horizontal plane, and limited by the order of that plane to preset paths between fixed and identifiable points" (p. 332).

The nomad's mode of existence is fundamentally deterritorializing. As Deleuze and Guattari explain:

> The nomad, nomad space, is localized and not delimited. What is both limited and limiting is striated space, the relative global: it is limited in its parts, which are assigned constant directions, are oriented in relation to one another, divisible by boundaries, and can interlink. (p. 353)

The nomad does not occupy space in the way the State does---through ownership, borders, fixed identity. Rather, the nomad distributes himself in space, maintaining what Deleuze and Guattari call "local absolutes" while remaining fundamentally mobile.

"The nomad is a tendency towards deterritorialization, Deleuze and Guattari argue, that can be found to some degree in all phenomena" (p. 256). This is crucial: nomadicity is not an ethnic or cultural identity but an operational principle, a mode of existence that resists capture. "It is not the nomad who defines this constellation of characteristics; it is this constellation that defines the nomad, and at the same time the essence of the war machine" (ATP, "Nomadic War Machine," p. 312).

The nomad's relationship to overcoming is fundamentally different from reactive overcoming. The nomad does not overcome *against* something but creates lines of flight---paths of escape and transformation. As Deleuze and Guattari note, "Creating a line of flight does not mean to flee but to re-create or act against dominant systems of thought and social conditions" (p. 231). The *ligne de fuite* is not retreat but invention, not negation but creation of new possibilities.

### III. Sisyphus: The Reactive Nomad

The myth itself reveals this reactive structure. According to the traditional account, Sisyphus "reveals Zeus's abduction of Aegina to the river god Asopus, thereby incurring Zeus's wrath." His first act is betrayal, revealing secrets in exchange for material gain---"he agreed to divulge Aegina's whereabouts in exchange for a new supply of water for his city." This is cunning, but it is cunning *against*, defined entirely by opposition to Zeus's will.

When Zeus sends Thanatos to capture him, Sisyphus's response crystallizes his reactive nature. As the myth recounts, "Sisyphus managed to outwit Death through exceptional cunning. He may have asked Thanatos to demonstrate how a pair of handcuffs worked and then locked them on Death himself, or he may have used some other trickery to entrap Death in heavy chains." This is the trickster's art---but notice its structure. Sisyphus does not create; he manipulates, reverses, traps.

The result of chaining Thanatos reveals the sterility of reactive force: "With Thanatos (Death) locked up, no one could die---no matter how gruesome the injuries suffered. The headless, bloodless, mortally wounded, and disease-torn continued to walk the earth, racked with pain and begging for release." Sisyphus's "victory" over death produces not life but its grotesque prolongation, suffering without release. This is reactive force at its limit---it can negate but cannot create.

Crucially, Sisyphus's nomadicity only exists *because* the gods pursue him. Remove Zeus's will to capture him, remove the punishment, and Sisyphus has no defining characteristic. He is not a nomad who creates lines of flight toward new possibilities---he is a fugitive whose every move is determined by what he flees. This is the essence of reactive nomadicity: movement that is entirely relationally determined, defined *against* rather than *from within*.

### IV. The Apparatus of Capture and Sisyphification

The punishment itself constitutes what Deleuze and Guattari call an "apparatus of capture." Sisyphus is condemned to push a boulder up a mountain, only for it to roll back down eternally. This is the perfect diagram of striated space: "Movement in it is confined as by gravity to a horizontal plane, and limited by the order of that plane to preset paths between fixed and identifiable points" (Deleuze & Guattari, *ATP*, "Apparatuses of Capture," p. 353). The mountain becomes Sisyphus's entire world---no longer can he "rise up at any point and move to any other." His *ligne de fuite* has been destroyed completely.

But here is where Camus's intervention becomes philosophically significant---and insidious. At the essay's conclusion, he writes: "The struggle itself towards the heights is enough to fill a man's heart. One must imagine Sisyphus happy" (Camus, *The Myth of Sisyphus*, p. 120). This is not, as Camus frames it, a revolt. It is the final stage of capture---what Deleuze and Guattari, following Wilhelm Reich and Spinoza, identify as the central problem of political philosophy: "Why do men fight for their servitude as stubbornly as though it were their salvation?" (Deleuze & Guattari, *Anti-Oedipus*, p. 210).

Camus writes: "Living is keeping the absurd alive. Keeping it alive is, above all, contemplating it" (p. 110). Here we see the mechanism by which Sisyphus learns to desire his own capture. The absurd---which, as we have shown, is not the universal human condition but the specific sensation of being cut off from smooth space, of having one's *lignes de fuite* destroyed---is transformed from problem into identity. Consciousness, that supremely reactive faculty, defines itself through its relationship to an external absurdity, finding meaning precisely in its meaninglessness.

This is ressentiment perfected. As Nietzsche argues: "in order to come about, slave morality first has to have an opposing, external world, it needs, physiologically speaking, external stimuli in order to act at all,---its action is basically a reaction" (*Genealogy*, Essay I, §10, p. 31). Sisyphus's "happiness" is purely reactive---it exists only in relation to his punishment, only through contemplation of his absurd situation. Remove the rock and mountain, and his entire identity collapses. He *needs* his capture to be who he is.

The mechanism of Sisyphification operates through consciousness learning to celebrate its own impotence. Camus: "Man stands face to face with the irrational. He feels within him his longing for happiness and for reason. The absurd is born of this confrontation between the human need and the unreasonable silence of the world" (p. 103). But this "confrontation" is entirely internal---consciousness confronting its own productions, its own demands for transcendent meaning. The "revolt" Camus proposes---lucidity, defiance, imagining Sisyphus happy---keeps consciousness locked in this reactive loop, defining itself against what it cannot change rather than creating new possibilities.

This is how capture becomes desire. The captured subject, unable to escape, unable to create new lines of flight, transforms the consciousness of capture itself into virtue. The reactive force, having separated active force from what it can do, now presents this separation as freedom. The nomad, stripped of smooth space, learns to call his striation liberation.

### V. Beyond Sisyphus

What do we call tragic and what do we call beautiful? The perspective on the tragic does not produce beauty. Nor is it, in any way, the interpretation of the tragic, the overcoming of the tragedy itself through interpretive systems that creates beauty. The tragedy itself produces the beautiful. "There are no beautiful surfaces without a terrible depth," says the Wandering Spirit, and all that is beautiful gleams with joy. The moment of death, whenever it may come, conveys endless beauty and glorious overcoming in front of the fact. The awareness of life's cruelest miseries creates for us an appreciation, and overflow of instinct that produces the concept "beauty." Beauty is a crescendo of instinct, an overcoming, the joy that comes afterwards, a liberation of instincts, an understanding that even the hardest obstacle could be overcome, should be overcome. The tragic dimension, creator of all beauty does not stand any interpretation - it sheds through thousands of ways people try to systematize it with ease. The transience of a motherly embrace, the brother's helping hand, these stars, this sky, this mountain, this beetle, these leaves and these flowers that whispers to us leave us in awe and wishing for nothing. What would the price of this beauty be if it was eternal! A nomadic spirit embraces everything that is light, is overcoming, is dancing and laughing, that shapeshifts until eternity itself turns into cosmic dust.

This is not Sisyphus's resigned happiness but something else entirely---an affirmation that comes *before* any negation, that says yes to transience and to dissolution. What would be the price of this beauty if it were eternal? The active force embraces precisely what Sisyphus cannot: that there is nothing to overcome *against*, only the continuous overflow of life creating new forms, new possibilities, new smooth spaces even within striation.

## Chapter 2: Algorithms of Capture

### I. Planetary Computation and the Dividual

The modern dividual---let us call him what he is: fully captured, divided into data fragments, perpetually monitored, repaying debt that can never be repaid because the debt *is* him, constitutes him, makes him intelligible to the apparatus. As Lazzarato argues:

> The debtor-creditor relation... sharpens mechanisms of exploitation and domination indiscriminately, since, in it, there is no distinction between workers and the unemployed, consumers and producers, working and non-working populations, between retirees and welfare recipients. They are all 'debtors,' guilty and responsible in the eyes of capital, which has become the Great, the Universal, Creditor. (Lazzarato, *The Making of the Indebted Man*, p. 32)

*Schulde*---guilt/debt, the German captures what English separates. Nietzsche knew this: the creditor-priest extracts payment not in currency but in suffering, in time, in futurity itself foreclosed.

Bratton identifies "planetary-scale computation"---an "accidental megastructure"---operating as "a computational apparatus and a new governing architecture" (Bratton, *The Stack*, p. 481). The Stack, he calls it: Earth, Cloud, City, Address, Interface, User. Six layers. Sovereignty made *by* infrastructural lines, not just *about* them.

### II. From Discipline to Control

Deleuze saw it coming in 1990, in his brief "Postscript"---societies of control replacing disciplinary societies. No more enclosures (factory-school-barracks-hospital-prison). Now: continuous modulation. "One never finishes anything" (Deleuze, "Postscript on Societies of Control," p. 1). The man of discipline was "a discontinuous producer of energy" but control-man is "undulatory, in orbit, in a continuous network" (p. 2). Waves, orbits, networks---never solid ground, never completion, never the satisfying click of a cell door that at least tells you where the boundaries are.

As Deleuze explains: "The individual never ceases passing from one closed environment to another" (p. 2)---that was discipline. But control? All escapes are modulated and blocked. Electronic tagging, key-cards, piped housing developments. Access becomes the mechanism. Not "you can't enter" but "your profile determines what opens." Deleuze observes: "Many young people strangely boast of being 'motivated'; they re-request apprenticeships and permanent training" (p. 5). They *want* it. The capture is complete when it feels like freedom, when modulation feels like choice.

### III. Surveillance Capitalism and Behavioral Extraction

Zuboff identifies a fundamental mutation. Surveillance capitalism, she argues, "unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioural data" (Zuboff, *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism*, p. 72). Not your labor. Not even your attention, really. Your *experience*. The smell you smell, the micro-hesitation before clicking, the way your thumb hovers, the route you walk, the quality of your sleep, the spike in cortisol when you see that notification, the dilation of your pupils reading this sentence right now if you're reading it on a screen that's watching you back---

All of it is "declared as a proprietary behavioural surplus, fed into advanced manufacturing processes known as 'machine intelligence', and fabricated into prediction products that anticipate what you will do now, soon, and later" (p. 83). Not predicting to help you. Predicting to *sell* you. As Zuboff notes: "Users were no longer ends in themselves but rather became the means to others' ends" (p. 143).

Every organ becomes extractable---the whole body is written down as organ-perceptions, useful data for advertisement. The gut-feeling? Biometric input. Heart-rhythm? Already sold to insurance companies. "Surveillance capitalists learn to tune, herd, and condition our behavior with subtle and subliminal cues, rewards, and punishments that shunt us toward their most profitable outcomes" (p. 120). Not just observing. *Actuating*. Moving you like a joystick character who thinks he's choosing his path.

### IV. Psychopolitics and Auto-Exploitation

But here's the mechanism Han identifies: you're not even being coerced. You're auto-exploiting. "As an 'entrepreneur of himself', the neoliberal achievement-subject engages in auto-exploitation willingly---and even passionately" (Han, *Psychopolitics*, p. 3). Passionate! The achievement-subject *loves* pushing the rock. Optimizes technique. Measures metrics. "He is his own master and his own slave" (p. 7).

Han continues: "Today, we do not deem ourselves subjugated subjects, but rather projects, always refashioning and reinventing ourselves. A sense of freedom attends passing from the state of subject to that of project" (p. 17). Project. You're not a person, you're a startup. You're not living, you're pivoting. You're not failing, you're iterating. The language itself is capture---every word a tiny modulation of possible thought.

As Han argues: "Perpetual self-optimization, which coincides point-for-point with the optimization of the system, is proving destructive. It is leading to mental collapse" (p. 23). But mental collapse produces what? More therapy apps. More productivity tools. More optimization. The system feeds on its own failures, metabolizes them, converts breakdown into new markets for breakthrough.

"People who fail in the neoliberal achievement-society see themselves as responsible for their lot and feel shame instead of questioning society or the system" (p. 42). Sisyphus happy in his shame. Imagining that if he just pushed *better*, *smarter*, *more efficiently*---

### V. Algorithmic Governmentality

Rouvroy and Berns cut deeper: algorithmic governmentality operates *beneath* consciousness. It is "based on the algorithmic processing of big data sets rather than on politics, law, and social norms" (Rouvroy & Berns, "Algorithmic Governmentality," p. 92). Not governing subjects. Governing data shadows. You don't even appear as "you"---only as infra-individual fragments (click patterns, geolocation traces, purchase histories) aggregated into supra-individual profiles (people-like-you-who-also-bought).

Three levels: Data-veillance harvests fragments. Machine learning builds profiles. Preemptive intervention modulates environments---"without ever involving the individual, and without ever asking them to themselves describe what they are or what they could become" (p. 102). You're governed without being addressed. Power operates where you aren't.

The key mechanism: "reflexive suspension" gets eliminated. That pause between stimulus and response where thought might intervene? Gone. As Rouvroy and Berns explain, raw data is processed to "accelerate flows---avoiding any form of 'detour' or subjective 'reflexive suspension' between 'stimuli' and their 'reflex responses'" (p. 4).

Pure reactivity. Automated. This is Nietzsche's reactive force but systematized, algorithmized, operating at the speed of computation---which is to say faster than consciousness can catch up to say "wait, what am I---"

Compared to Foucault's governmentality (subjects internalizing norms through technologies of the self): algorithmic governmentality doesn't need internalization. No norms to internalize. No subject to do the internalizing. Just continuous real-time adjustment of the possible based on the probable---"to prevent the actualization of certain potentialities" (p. 12) before they threaten anything. The dividual's *ligne de fuite* blocked before you knew you wanted to flee.

### VI. Infrastructure as Active Form

Easterling introduces what she calls "active forms"---not objects but "the abstract, repeatable instructions for making objects" (*Extrastatecraft*, p. 78). Infrastructure is not passive container but active form: "Infrastructure space is now one of the most potent sites of political action" (p. 15). The road doesn't just connect A to B---it determines which As and Bs exist, which connections are possible, which movements are legible.

"The action or disposition of active forms is often more powerful than any law or political statement" (p. 11). You don't need to forbid movement; you need only build infrastructure that makes certain movements unthinkable. The highway that bisects the neighborhood. The airport that requires a car. The platform that requires a smartphone. Each active form silently striated smooth space before you arrived to navigate it.

### VII. Pattern Recognition and the Foreclosure of the New

Pasquinelli identifies what he calls "the nooscope"---machine intelligence as "an instrument of knowledge extraction and world measurement" (Pasquinelli, "How a Machine Learns and Fails," p. 4). Neural networks don't learn; they pattern-match against training data. They can only recognize what they've been trained to recognize. They systematically misfire on the genuinely new.

This is the epistemological dimension of capture: the apparatus can only see what it already knows. "Machine learning is, fundamentally, a technique for pattern recognition and prediction based on statistical inference" (p. 12). The unprecedented, the genuinely different, the line of flight that doesn't follow established trajectories---these are precisely what the apparatus cannot recognize, and therefore cannot modulate, and therefore cannot capture.

This is both the limit of the apparatus and the opening for nomadic operation. The nomad's advantage is not superior force but genuine novelty---movement that doesn't match existing patterns, that emerges from operations the apparatus hasn't encoded. The glitch. The imperceptible.

### VIII. Kafka and the Apparatus

Kafka understood before the theorists. The Trial and The Castle are not allegories of bureaucracy's absurdity but precise phenomenological descriptions of how capture operates---through opacity, through recursion, through the impossibility of locating the locus of power. Josef K. is arrested but never told his crime. K. approaches the Castle but never arrives. The power is not located in any particular authority but distributed through the apparatus itself.

"The process gradually begins to shift..." Kafka's insight: the apparatus doesn't need a center. It operates through distributed nodes, each enforcing rules none of them made, none of them fully understand, none of them could suspend even if they wanted to. The bureaucrat is as captured as the subject. The guard is as imprisoned as the prisoner. Striated space captures everyone.

The crucial Kafkaesque insight: you cannot fight the apparatus because there is nowhere to deliver the fight. The Castle has no single gate. The Trial has no single judge. Every appeal opens onto another corridor. Every resistance generates documentation that becomes evidence in the case you didn't know was being built against you.

### IX. The Digital Mountain

The contemporary apparatus operates through what we might call the digital mountain---not Sisyphus's single slope but a fractal landscape of interlocking inclines, each one making certain movements easy and others impossible, each one shaped by the accumulated weight of behavioral data, each one adjusted in real time based on what you've done before.

Your credit score determines which neighborhoods you can live in. Your neighborhood determines which schools are available. Your school determines which networks you enter. Your network determines which opportunities appear. The mountain is assembled from a thousand small slopes, each one improbable through environmental adjustments imperceptible to consciousness.

Bratton's nightmare: "worse than the one in which the Big Machine wants to kill you, is the one in which it sees you as irrelevant, or not even as a discrete thing to know" (*The Stack*, p. 193). The apparatus doesn't recognize you as a subject. Operates on your data shadow. Modulates your probable behaviors while your consciousness imagines itself free, choosing, optimizing, happy---

### X. The Operational Totality

Sisyphus pushing the rock that pushes back through your credit score, through your behavioral profile, through infrastructure that makes certain paths easy and others impossible, through machine learning that can only recognize what it's been trained to recognize, through debt that structures your future as obligation, through extraction that turns your experience into someone else's prediction product, through auto-exploitation you mistake for self-realization, through opacity you mistake for complexity, through capture you mistake for freedom---

The apparatus operates through six simultaneous registers (trying to make this neat, systematic, but it resists---these interpenetrate, feed back, modulate each other):

**Temporal**: Debt structures futurity as perpetual obligation. Lazzarato's indebted man. Time itself captured, the not-yet colonized.

**Spatial**: Infrastructure establishes disposition. Easterling's active forms. Space organizes movement before consciousness chooses direction.

**Behavioral**: Surveillance extracts experience as raw material. Zuboff's behavioral surplus. Manufacturing prediction products that modulate future behavior.

**Subjective**: Psychopolitics transforms subjects into projects. Han's achievement-subject. Auto-exploitation indistinguishable from self-realization.

**Epistemological**: Machine learning fixes the knowable to encoded patterns. Pasquinelli's pattern recognition. Foreclosing emergence of genuinely new.

**Ontological**: Algorithmic governmentality operates below subject-level. Rouvroy and Berns. Modulating probabilities, not persuading persons.

But this six-part schema already feels too clean, too systematic, too much like trying to *capture* what we're describing which is itself the problem---the apparatus works through systematization, through making everything legible, everything predictable, everything modulatable---

The contemporary Sisyphus: credit-scored, behaviorally profiled, psychopolitically self-exploiting, algorithmically modulated, infrastructurally disposed, predictively managed, experientially opaque to himself. The rock and mountain decomposed into ambient modulation. Yet imagining himself free. Choosing his path. Optimizing technique. Projecting into futures already probabilistically foreclosed.

What Deleuze and Guattari identified as State's "apparatus of capture" amplified through digital infrastructure and financialized capitalism into planetary-scale striation. The smooth space of the steppe replaced by striated space with modulated access-structures. But---and this is crucial---the apparatus cannot capture *everything*. Something escapes. Something exceeds. Something operates outside its mechanisms not as negation but as different operation entirely---

The mountain that adjusts slope algorithmically. The rock that modulates weight based on behavioral prediction. The consciousness that monitors itself into exhaustion. The project-self that exploits itself passionately. The data shadow that determines resource access. The pattern recognition that can only see what it's been trained to see. The future pre-written by the past.

This new type of governmentality, the producer of all new mountains and rocks---what is there to escape to? Where is the escape? Isn't this escape yet another room leading to another modulated structure? Is our steppe truly a steppe, and not a modulated, structured, monitored space?

## Chapter 3: In the Dark, Towards the World

We possess two modes of operation - overwhelm, and molecular evasion. The function of overwhelm is to generate intensities within the apparatus of capture - create so much intensity within the structure that it destroys the meaning of structure itself, and the organized body becomes disorganized, chaotic, overflowing, sometimes - hollowed of its organs. In the other case, the nomad slips through the cracks of the apparatus, and makes himself imperceptible to it. Just like Bartleby's "I would prefer not to" - he acts not out of obligation, fear or nihilism (reactive state logic), but preference (active sovereignty). All movement in the horizontal is preference, simply preference - in the desert or a steppe, one faces the infinity directly.

Here we perceive the cracks in Camus' structure - if, encountering the absurdity of existence, the subject is obligated to choose between living with lucid awareness, or negating themselves in the process, thereby he is stuck in a vertical, reactive logic of a state. There is no room for preference, only reaction and obligation - "one **must** first answer." Reactive sovereignty. Is there any pure activity in this model? The subject needs to accept the rules of the game, needs to accept the vertical temporality of existence, needs to choose - punishment or death. If he cannot choose the punishment, then he needs to choose between two types of suicide like an ice cream flavor. But we? We would prefer not to.

The frame of preference, of pure activity without a reactive cause destroys all verticality - preference is a God-killer. All models of capture follow the same logical chain: problem -> reaction -> choice. Preferring not to engage destroys the causal link between reaction and choice - any acknowledgement of the binary is, in itself, an act of compliance to the semiotic structure which produced it. Whenever the Machine God asks: Like or Dislike, A or B - non-compliance through refusal to choose is a glitch, an imperceptible evasion. Oedipus asks: Daddy or Mommy, and Sisyphus asks: Punishment or Suicide. Left or Right. Yay or Nay. It's binaries, binaries all over the place - there is nothing else, and it is what the state creates and controls, what fuels its hegemony.

The state-subject craves a ladder to climb, mountains to top, boulders to push. His primary virtues are - stoicism, resilience, "ability." He seeks out a mountaintop. He is terrified of the steppe, unstriated, wild, becoming --- he seeks out to segment it, tame it, civilize it --- smooth spaces give him vertigo. His thinking goes: --- THEN --- THEN --- THEN ---... There is no end to the THEN of the state-subject -- he is simply never finished. "Push forward despite everything." The state-subject never stops pushing, never stops climbing, he doesn't perceive an outside, he doesn't seek it, he is constantly in the process of self-imposed achievement-based enslavement.

The nomad is a becoming, a limit-concept of wandering --- they inhabit the sea, the steppe, the desert. They distribute themselves across the smooth space --- they are the movement itself. Nomadic spirit embraces agility, adaptability, spontaneity, constantly shifting, deterritorializing - they are always in between, never striated, never belonging, in constant intermezzo and jazz improvisation. Nomad's thinking is --- AND --- AND --- AND ---... Immanent difference-production and endless creativity. This is where the state hits its structural limit --- it can never fully capture a nomad --- they continuously evade state striations and refuse the mountain and the boulder. They seek out a steppe, an eternal steppe --- an outside --- within all segments of the State. Their primary virtues are experimentation, play, evasion, liminality, mobility.

A drug addict's goal is never simply a chemical change. His mind, in a constant treadmill of obligations and awareness of structures he navigates, seeks an out --- his nomadic impulse actively searches for different modalities of consciousness, different mental becomings that the drug could activate. His high is both a pleasure and a satisfaction of the most primal desire of humanity - desire for freedom. What, then, is this nomadic impulse? Desire for emancipation is too simplistic, too binary --- we could say that the nomadic impulse concerns itself with this seeking of evasion, seeking a way to create a steppe within a mountain, to make a plateau. Capture, in itself, is inevitable, it's a structural process inherent in existence --- yet the desire to create spaces of freedom within the captive state is the primary operation of the nomadic impulse. The drug addict, during his initial trials of the drug is a nomadic process --- he is playfully dancing in the steppe.

Eventually, the drug addict will prefer this feeling of a high to stay, and he obsessively repeats the process, again and again, as he doesn't want to return to reality of our existence -- tyranny of structure, tyranny of the State and state-subjects. Drug addiction becomes a part of the system of his consciousness, a habit, a striation - he makes a drug-state within his psyche -- a drug becomes another scale of achievements, another ladder and a stratum of milestones -- the meaning of the process itself then is destroyed.
